The petitioner invoked the constitutional jurisdiction of the High Court
under Article 199 of the Constitution, challenging departmental

ISSUE
Whether the denial of an opportunity of hearing before an adverse
departmental order violates the right to a fair trial and due process
guaranteed under Article 10-A of the Constitution, and whether
constitutional jurisdiction under Article 199 was properly invoked in the
absence of an alternate adequate remedy.

REASONING (illustrative)
The Court reiterated that Article 10-A guarantees a fair trial and due
process in all proceedings, including departmental/administrative
proceedings with civil consequences, not merely criminal trials. Where an
order is passed in flagrant violation of natural justice, the existence
of a departmental appeal does not, by itself, oust the writ jurisdiction
under Article 199.
