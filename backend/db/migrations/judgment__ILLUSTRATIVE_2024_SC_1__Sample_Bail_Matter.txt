Court: Supreme Court of Pakistan (illustrative)
Case: Sample Petitioner v. The State (illustrative bail petition)
Bench: Illustrative Bench

SUMMARY OF FACTS
The petitioner sought post-arrest bail in a case registered under Section
302 PPC read with Section 34 PPC, alleging that the accused caused the
death of the complainant's brother following a property dispute. The
petitioner contended that his name was introduced in the FIR through a
belated supplementary statement, and that no independent eyewitness
supported the prosecution's version at the bail stage.

ISSUE
Whether the case falls within the prohibitory clause of Section 497 CrPC,
and whether the material on record makes out reasonable grounds to
believe the petitioner is guilty of an offence punishable with death, so
as to justify continued incarceration pending trial.

REASONING (illustrative)
The Court observed that while Section 302 PPC ordinarily attracts the
prohibitory clause of Section 497 CrPC, bail cannot be declined merely on
the seriousness of the allegation where the record itself raises a
genuine doubt about the petitioner's specific role. Delay in naming an
accused, without plausible explanation, is a relevant factor at the bail
stage under Section 497 CrPC, distinct from its weight at trial.

HELD
Bail was allowed, subject to furnishing solvent surety, on the ground
that further inquiry into the petitioner's guilt was called for within
the meaning of Section 497 CrPC.
