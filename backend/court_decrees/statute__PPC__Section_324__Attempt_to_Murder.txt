Pakistan Penal Code, 1860 — Section 324: Attempt to Commit Qatl-e-Amd (Attempt to Murder)

Section 324 PPC criminalizes an act done with the intention or knowledge, and under such circumstances, that if death were caused, the offender would be guilty of qatl-e-amd (Section 302). Punishment prescribed is imprisonment for life, or imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to ten years, and shall also be liable to a fine; and if hurt is caused to any person by such act, the offender shall also be liable to the punishment provided for the hurt caused.

Key points:
- The prosecution must establish intention or knowledge equivalent to that required for qatl-e-amd — mere assault or grievous hurt without the requisite intent/knowledge does not attract Section 324; lesser hurt provisions (Sections 332–338 PPC) may apply instead.
- Circumstantial factors commonly examined by courts: nature of the weapon used, part of the body targeted, number and severity of injuries, prior enmity/motive, and medico-legal evidence.
- Section 324 PPC is non-bailable, cognizable, and triable by the Court of Sessions.

Practical relevance: A frequently invoked section in FIRs involving firearm injuries or serious assault where the complainant alleges intent to kill; defence strategy often focuses on challenging the requisite intention/knowledge and the medico-legal evidence (nature/location of injury) to argue for a lesser offence.
