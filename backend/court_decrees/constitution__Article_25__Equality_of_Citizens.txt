Constitution of the Islamic Republic of Pakistan, 1973 — Article 25: Equality of Citizens

Article 25 provides that all citizens are equal before law and are entitled to equal protection of law, that there shall be no discrimination on the basis of sex alone, and permits the State to make special provisions for the protection of women and children.

Summary of legal effect:
- "Equal protection of law" prohibits arbitrary or unreasonable classification by the State — any classification must have a rational nexus with the object sought to be achieved (the classic "reasonable classification" test applied by Pakistani superior courts, drawing on subcontinental constitutional jurisprudence).
- Article 25(2) specifically bars discrimination "on the basis of sex alone" — meaning sex-based classification is permissible only where justified by a real and substantial distinction relevant to the purpose of the law (e.g., maternity-related protections), not as a blanket bar on any differential treatment.
- Article 25(3) is an enabling provision allowing the State to enact protective legislation for women and children without that legislation being struck down as discriminatory against men.
- Frequently invoked together with Article 27 (non-discrimination in services) in service matters, and with family/labour law challenges.

Practical relevance: Commonly cited in service law disputes (promotions, recruitment criteria), challenges to discriminatory administrative rules, and family law matters involving differential treatment.
