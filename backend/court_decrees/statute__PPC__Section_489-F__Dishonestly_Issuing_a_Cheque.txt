Pakistan Penal Code, 1860 — Section 489-F: Dishonestly Issuing a Cheque

Section 489-F PPC criminalizes issuing a cheque towards repayment of a loan or fulfilment of an obligation, which is dishonoured on presentation due to insufficiency of funds or because it exceeds the amount arranged to be paid from the account, where the cheque was issued dishonestly. Punishment: imprisonment which may extend to three years, or with fine, or with both.

Key points frequently litigated:
- The prosecution must show dishonest intention at the time of issuing the cheque, not merely that the cheque bounced — a bona fide business dispute (e.g., cheque given as security, post-dated cheque for a transaction later disputed, stop-payment due to a genuine dispute over goods/services) is a common defence.
- Essential ingredients generally examined by courts: (i) issuance of a cheque, (ii) towards repayment of a loan or fulfilment of an obligation, (iii) dishonour on presentation for insufficiency of funds/exceeding arranged amount, and (iv) dishonest intention.
- The offence is non-bailable but courts have granted bail in appropriate 489-F cases where triable facts require determination at trial and the accused is not a flight risk, particularly where the dispute appears to be of a civil/contractual nature.
- Limitation: a complaint must generally be filed within the period prescribed and after the statutory notice/demand (where the relevant procedure under the Negotiable Instruments Act / PPC scheme requires it) — defence often examines compliance with any such procedural prerequisite.

Practical relevance: One of the most common sections in cheque-dishonour criminal complaints in Pakistan; central to legal notice replies and FIR/complaint analysis in financial disputes.
