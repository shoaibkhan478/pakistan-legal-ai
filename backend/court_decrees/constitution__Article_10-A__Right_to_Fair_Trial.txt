Constitution of the Islamic Republic of Pakistan, 1973 — Article 10-A: Right to Fair Trial

Article 10-A (inserted by the Constitution (18th Amendment) Act, 2010) guarantees that for the determination of a person's civil rights and obligations or in any criminal charge against a person, that person shall be entitled to a fair trial and due process.

Summary of legal effect:
- This is a fundamental right applicable to BOTH civil and criminal proceedings — it is not limited to criminal trials.
- "Fair trial and due process" has been read by superior courts to include: the right to be heard, the right to an independent and impartial tribunal, adequate opportunity to present one's case and evidence, the right to cross-examine, and reasoned decisions.
- Article 10-A is frequently invoked to challenge trials conducted by special/military courts or tribunals where standard due-process safeguards were allegedly bypassed, and to challenge ex-parte proceedings or denial of opportunity of hearing in departmental/administrative proceedings.
- It works alongside Article 4 (right of individuals to be dealt with in accordance with law) and Article 9 (security of person) as part of the constitutional due-process framework.

Practical relevance: Commonly cited in appeals and constitutional petitions alleging that a trial court, tribunal, or departmental inquiry failed to give adequate opportunity of hearing, denied cross-examination, or otherwise violated due process.
